PyTutor: Empowering Equitable Education Pathways in Computing with Generative AI

Present

PyTutor is an AI tutoring platform for students learning introductory Python programming. It is currently deployed in courses across MIT, Georgia State University, and Quinsigammond Community College and is being actively used by hundreds of students. The platform leverages a fully-featured coding environment and a large language model to provide personalized educational support to students.

Key contributions include advanced retrieval systems for the RAG pipeline, personalized feedback features based on student classroom performance, and the development of an evaluation framework for understanding AI tutor behavior.

Robo-Advisor for the Korean Individual Retirement Pension Funds

2024

In 2023, the Financial Services Commission of South Korea announced the integration of quantitative approaches to investment management and advisory services into the national retirement pension system. Under this policy, "robo-advisors" - quantitative, model-driven investment systems approved by the Robo Advisor Test Bed Center - are authorized to provide investment advisory and management services for Individual Retirement Pension (IRP) accounts beginning in the summer of 2024.

In this context, we developed a semi-systematic, algorithmic trading strategy based on market cycles and seasonality theories. The strategy provides asset allocations across stock market indices and the bond market with human intervention for monthly rebalancing. The system successfully passed the six-month evaluation conducted by the Robo Advisor Test Bed Center and was officially launched in November 2024 with an initial capital of $6.7K. Since launch, the strategy has achieved a 15.58% annualized return.
